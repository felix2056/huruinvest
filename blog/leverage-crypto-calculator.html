What Is Leverage Crypto Trading?

Leverage crypto trading refers to when you borrow capital to increase the amount of cryptocurrency that you can invest. The leverage that you have is listed either as a ratio or a number followed by “x.” So, you may see 100:1 or 100x, but they mean the same thing. 

How Does Leverage Trading Work in Crypto?

When you leverage crypto, you will start by choosing an exchange that offers margin trading for crypto. You will have to deposit some capital. Then, you can choose your leverage. You will also usually have to keep a margin threshold of crypto in your account. If you don’t have that threshold, the platform will close your position. 

10x, 20x, 25x, 100x – What Do These Mean?

10x, 20x, and other figures (or 10:1, 20:1, etc.) refers to the amount of leverage you have. Think of it as how far each dollar you invest goes. So, if you leverage Bitcoin by investing $100 and have 10:1 leverage, you can invest the equivalent of $1,000. If you had $100 and 100:1 leverage, you could invest the equivalent of $10,000.

Leverage Crypto Trading in the UK

Leverage trading platforms for cryptocurrency in the UK include eToro, Libertex, Avatrade, XTB, CEX.IO, Binance, Pocket Option, and Pepperstone. 

Leverage Crypto Trading in the US

You can trade cryptocurrency with leverage in the United States, but you can’t trade crypto CFDs with leverage as a US resident. Some options for trading crypto with leverage in the US include BitMEX, Bybit, Binance, Kraken, Bityard, Poloniex, Bitfinex, KuCoin, and Prime BIT. 

What Happens If Things Go Wrong

The big reason to be cautious when you leverage crypto is the risks if things go wrong. Because you are trading more than your capital, the potential losses are large. Remember that your potential losses increase at the same ratio as your potential gains. 

Unless you engage in crypto margin trading on a platform that doesn’t let your balance drop below zero, you could lose more than you invest. You would then have to pay back all the funds you borrowed from the exchange. 

Because of the risk involved, margin trading cryptocurrency is not a good idea for beginners. You should only consider it if you are already an experienced crypto trader or you don’t mind losing significantly more than your initial investment. 

Margin Trading Tips

While there are risks associated with leverage trading, the following tips will help you manage them. 

Start With Low Leverage

When you start margin trading cryptocurrency, stick to lower levels of leverage. This will minimize your potential losses as you get used to this type of trading. 

Use Stop-Loss Orders

These orders are designed to limit your potential losses. Your positions are automatically closed if the price reaches a certain (low) point that you specify. These minimize your losses if the market drops rapidly. 

Use Take-Profit Orders

Take-profit orders are there to secure your profits. They will automatically close if your profits get to the value you specify. They let you keep your earnings if the market rises and then falls. 

Never Risk More Than You Are Willing to Lose

A general rule for cryptocurrency investment is to never invest more than you can afford to lose. This guideline is because of the volatility of cryptocurrency. But it is even more important with leverage trading because of the risk involved. 

Choose a Platform with Negative Balance Protection

Without negative balance protection, you can end up owing more than your initial investment. Choosing a platform with this negative balance protection prevents this. Simply put, platforms that offer this will not let your account go negative. If it is negative, it gets reset to $0. This strategy limits your potential losses. 

Use a Demo Account First

Some platforms offer demo accounts for you to practice before you invest real funds. You should always take advantage of this before using margin trading. These demo accounts let you get to know the platform and test various margin trading strategies without risk. 

When to Use Leverage

You should only use leverage if you understand the risks and are an experienced cryptocurrency trader familiar with general crypto trading strategies. Otherwise, stick to trading without leverage. 

Is Leveraging Crypto a Good Idea?

You will have to decide for yourself whether leveraging crypto is a good idea. It is risky, but some people are attracted to the potential profits. Beginners definitely shouldn’t consider it, but advanced traders are welcome to make their own decisions. That said, most people will agree that the risks outweigh the rewards. 

To help you decide, refresh your memory on some advantages and disadvantages of using leverage with crypto trading already mentioned: 

Advantages include: 

  • Your initial investment goes further. 
  • You can divide your investments across more exchanges with the help of leverage. 
  • You can use your capital with more flexibility. 
  • It can help you grow your portfolio more quickly. 
  • You can start with a very small amount. 
  • It can teach risk management strategies. 

Disadvantages include: 

  • Your losses can be much higher. 
  • Losses can occur very quickly. 
  • You may need to increase your margin balance. 
  • It is much riskier than trading without leverage. 
  • It is not suitable for beginners. 

Conclusion

While you can dramatically increase your potential profits by margin trading crypto, it also comes with significant risks. That is especially true if you aren’t an experienced trader. For most people, it will be smarter to skip margin trading cryptocurrency and instead invest their funds in an account managed by experts. The best accounts have expert-developed and monitored algorithms that take full advantage of the crypto market being open 24/7.
