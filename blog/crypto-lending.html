Crypto Lending: Definitive Guide for Beginners

November 28, 2022November 27, 2022 by haruinvest

Crypto assets have become collateral for new types of lending in recent years. Over traditional lending models, crypto lending offers a number of advantages and disadvantages.

Many people buy cryptocurrency intending to hold it for a while. This has led many people to wonder how to earn interest or passive income on their cryptocurrency. Crypto lending is one of the possible solutions, and it has grown in popularity. Now, many platforms offer cryptocurrency lending and borrowing.

What is crypto lending?

Recent years have seen the emergence of a new type of lending that uses crypto assets as collateral. Crypto lending offers several benefits over traditional lending models.

Firstly, crypto assets can be used as collateral without having to be sold by borrowers. Thus, borrowers can retain ownership of their assets while still accessing liquidity.

Secondly, crypto lending offers lower interest rates than traditional banks, reducing borrowing costs.

The lower loan minimums offered by crypto lending platforms make them more accessible to a wider range of borrowers than traditional banks.

These factors make crypto lending an appealing option for those looking to borrow against their digital assets.

How Does Crypto Lending Work?

The basics behind lending cryptocurrency are the same as lending any other type of asset. The lender temporarily gives the borrower a set amount of cryptocurrency. After a specified amount of time, the borrower returns the amount borrowed, along with interest.

Those who participate in cryptocurrency borrowing can own crypto for a short period of time without any hassles for urgent liquidation requirements and without having to sell their assets.

Crypto lenders, on the other hand, earn interest on cryptocurrency that would otherwise just sit in their wallets.

Collateral Ratio: Borrowers can’t Borrow More than They Have

It is important to understand one thing carefully. Borrowers usually need more assets than they need. As an example, if they want to borrow 1000 USDT, they will need to transfer 1400 USDT to the smart contract address for guaranteed repayment. Crypto lending solves the problem of repayment by preventing borrowers from borrowing more than they can afford.

In addition to credit checks and risk ratings, some DeFi platforms are also doing some innovations in this area to make the DeFi lending process more sustainable.

Is there any regulation on crypto lending?

Crypto lending has a lot of risks when it comes to regulation and legal structure. This situation can be exemplified by Blockfi, Celsius, FTX and so on.

How to Get a Crypto-Backed Loan in 3 steps

Borrowing and lending crypto is a very straightforward process.

  1. The borrower must decide the repayment date, collateral coin, and transfer collateral to the smart contract address.
  2. The personal loan is received by them.
  3. At the end of the period, they repay the loan.

What happens if you don’t pay back a crypto loan?

The collateral will be liquidated if the borrower fails to repay the loan. Alternatively, if the price of the coin used as collateral varies greatly and no longer guarantees the loan value, the collateral is automatically liquidated, and the additional amount is returned to the borrower.

There is no requirement that borrowers borrow the same coin with collateral. Ethereum, for example, can be used as collateral for USDT borrowing.

Is crypto lending safe?

Crypto lending is risky. Here’re the cons of crypto lending:

  • Price volatility risks
  • Counterparty risk
  • Platform insolvency
  • Custody and security concerns
  • Smart contract technology failures
  • Unclear cryptocurrency regulations

What is Decentralized Finance (DeFi) Lending?

The concept of decentralized finance (DeFi) refers to lending that occurs on decentralized platforms. Typically, these platforms are built on Ethereum and use smart contracts to facilitate lending.

Due to their decentralized nature, DeFi lending platforms are not subject to the same regulations as traditional financial institutions.

With this approach, loan products and services can be offered to a broader audience and costs can be reduced.

Since DeFi lending platforms are not subject to the same risk management standards as traditional loans, they also tend to have higher interest rates. Thus, DeFi lending can be a more profitable option for borrowers, but it is also more complicated and has a learning curve.

Top Crypto Lending Platforms

There are many lending platforms and crypto exchanges available today. However, not all platforms are created equal. Listed below are the top crypto lending platforms, according to user reviews.

Blockfi

BlockFi offers competitive interest rates and a wide range of features, making it one of the most popular lending platforms. In addition to offering users a flexible way to access liquidity, BlockFi also allows them to borrow against their crypto holdings.

Nexo

Another leading provider, Nexo, offers a similar service to BlockFi. Alternatively, Nexo offers instant crypto credit lines, which may be useful if you need funds quickly.

Coinloan

In addition to offering competitive rates and a variety of collateral options, CoinLoan is another popular option.

These are just a few of the top crypto lending platforms available today. Knowing which platform is right for you can be challenging with so many options available. The best platform will depend on your needs and your research, so consider your needs and do some research.

How to Lend Crypto?

In order to lend crypto, you will need to know which coin you want to lend, as well as the loan term. You can also choose the interest rate on some DeFi platforms. The exact process will vary slightly depending on your chosen crypto lending platform.

Liquidity is the key word. Imagine someone invested a large amount in Ethereum, but the price dropped due to the market situation.
