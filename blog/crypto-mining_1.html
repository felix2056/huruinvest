Crypto Mining: Quick Start Guide

November 28, 2022November 27, 2022 by haruinvest

Mining cryptocurrency creates new units of digital currency. Cryptocurrency is mined using powerful computers. The mining process rewards miners with new currency units, which they can exchange or use.

There are many ways to potentially earn cryptocurrency as a side hustle, and crypto mining companies or mining pools tend to be the first option that comes to mind. But before you decide to try crypto mining, you need to know some basics about it. This guide gives an overview of cryptocurrency mining, bitcoin mining, and altcoin mining.

What is Cryptocurrency Mining

Cryptocurrency mining is the process of creating new units of digital currency. The currency is mined using powerful computers that solve complex mathematical problems. The mining process rewards miners with new units of the currency, which they can then exchange for other currencies or use for purchases.

As the value of many digital currencies has skyrocketed in recent years, cryptocurrency mining has become increasingly popular. Climate change has been attributed to its energy-intensive nature. White House published a press release about Bitcoin and cryptocurrency’s electricity usage.

It is likely that mining will become more widespread as the price of cryptocurrencies continues to rise.

Why crypto mining is necessary?

Crypto mining requirements will vary depending on the system used by digital assets. Proof-of-work (PoW) requires mining; proof-of-stake does not.

For example, Bitcoin, Dogecoin, or Litecoin use a Proof-of-Work consensus, requiring miners to solve complex mathematical problems to add new blocks to the blockchain.

A full list of Proof-of-Work tokens can be found here.

In the PoW world, without miners, transactions would not be able to be verified and added to the blockchain, and the entire system would grind to a halt.

Mining cryptocurrencies has some advantages, such as the rewards mentioned above, but it also has some disadvantages.

The cost of electricity can be high when mining, for instance. The difficulty of mathematical problems increases as more people mine cryptocurrencies, so earning rewards takes more time and effort. In spite of these drawbacks, mining remains a crucial part of many cryptocurrencies.

What is Bitcoin Mining?

Bitcoin mining involves verifying and adding transaction records to the public ledger known as the blockchain. Blockchains contain a record of every bitcoin transaction ever made.

In exchange for solving math problems, bitcoin miners receive a certain number of bitcoins. It provides an incentive for people to mine bitcoins. Whenever someone sends a bitcoin, miners verify the transaction.

What are Bitcoin Mining pools?

As more and more people use Bitcoin, more computers are needed to verify transactions. This requires miners to work together in what is called ‘mining pools.’ Mining pools are a way for individual miners to work together and share resources in order to increase their chances of getting rewards:

Bitcoin block rewards consist of 6.25 newly generated coins per block. Every four years, a halvening event regulates the number of newly generated coins. During this halving event, the supply of newly generated bitcoins will be cut in half, and the supply will be tightened until all 21 million bitcoins have been mined. This is what we call: bitcoin halving. The next Bitcoin halving is scheduled to take place in 2024 at block 840,000. On Jul 08, 2024.

By working together in pools and sharing resources, miners can increase their chances of getting rewards.

There will only ever be 21 million Bitcoins mined, so it’s safe to say that Bitcoin mining is here to stay for a while!

Ethereum Mining

Ethereum shifted from the Proof-of-Work concept to the Proof-of-Stake concept. We published an article about this process which can be found here.

Keeping the blockchain network secure and validating transactions is better with Ethereum staking after switching to Proof-of-Stake. You can mine Ethereum using staking instead of hardware.

Alt Coin Mining

As mentioned, alt currency mining is a possibility for nearly any altcoin that has a Proof of Work consensus mechanism. Altcoins are subject to the same rules.

Is Crypto Mining Worth It?

Depending on who you ask, you may get a different answer as to whether crypto mining is worth it. When considering the potential profits, you also have to think about the costs of cryptocurrency mining. From the crypto mining rig to the excessive energy use, mining can come with high costs.

The answer will also depend on the value of the coin you mine. That point alone makes it hard to predict whether mining is worth it, as cryptocurrency prices are volatile.

How Much Can You Earn with Crypto Mining?

You do not earn cash at the end of the mining period, and the cost of the mining process will vary based on the complexity of the mathematical problems, the price of the token you are mining, the hardware required to solve mathematical problems, and the cost of electricity.

Therefore, earnings don’t have a fixed rate. You need to calculate it before making a wise investment, as it varies daily, even hourly.

Risks of Cryptocurrency Mining

Mining is a resource-intensive process that can have negative environmental and financial consequences.

Electricity is required for cryptocurrency mining, for example. Miners may have to operate at a loss to pay their energy bills. Climate change can also be caused by cryptocurrency mining. As a result of the energy used to power miners’ computers, greenhouse gases are produced, which contribute to global warming.

There are also financial risks associated with cryptocurrency mining. Tokens are earned by crypto miners, but bills are paid in cash. As a result, unexpected token price changes can lead to unexpected coincidences. As a result, miners may find themselves owing more money than they can repay if a cryptocurrency’s value decreases.

How to Start Crypto Mining

Before getting started with cryptocurrency mining, there are a few things to keep in mind. To begin with, make sure you do your research and understand the risks involved. Secondly, make sure you have access to cheap electricity. Last but not least, consider joining a mining pool, which will allow you to pool resources with other miners and increase your chances of success.

What Are the Requirements to Join Crypto Mining Pools?

To work with crypto mining pools, you will need to decide whether to invest in hardware or do cloud mining. Crypto mining can even be done using a mobile phone in some pools.

You will need to install some software to be part of the pool if you invest in hardware and build your own mining computer. If you want to use cloud mining, you will just need to sign up. Cloud mining has a very straightforward onboarding process.

Pools have different requirements. Choose the best option for you by searching and finding the appropriate pool to start with.

How Much Should You Invest to Start Crypto Mining?

There’s no one answer to this question since it can vary greatly depending on a number of factors, including the type of cryptocurrency being mined, the equipment being used, and the electricity costs in the area where the mining is taking place.

As the software does not cost anything, mining Bitcoin is technically free. Hardware and electricity expenses, however, are extremely expensive.

Based on the Visual Capitalist dataset, bitcoin mining costs on average $35,404.03 across 198 countries.

Mining pools allow multiple miners to pool their computing power, but each miner only receives a share of the Bitcoins mined.
