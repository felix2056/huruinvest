Stocks and Crypto Decoupling amid Russian War

March 8, 2022 by haruinvest

In recent months, cryptocurrencies have seen similar price movements as stocks, especially tech stocks, suggesting that crypto was being categorized as ‘risk assets’. But this situation changed as Russia launched its attack on Ukraine and the US, European Union (EU), and other countries enacted economic sanctions against Russia. How is the war affecting crypto as an investment asset?

Russian war spells ‘cloudy’ forecast for stocks and ‘sunny’ for Bitcoin

During the Russian war, crypto has moved onto a different path than stocks.

The price of Bitcoin (BTC) has risen since Russia attacked Ukraine. As of March 3rd, CoinMarketCap data shows the Bitcoin price exceeding $43,000, a 22% increase over the previous week. Other major cryptocurrencies tracked Bitcoin, recording double-digit growth. Ethereum (ETH) rose about 19% from a week ago, Binance Coin (BNB) increased by 20%, Ripple (XRP) climbed by 19%, Solana gained about 20%, and Terra’s Luna (LUNA), which also reported positive business performance, surged by a whopping 70%.

These increases happened at the same time as blue-chip and technology stocks weakened amid stronger risk-averse sentiment due to the war. Bitcoin started its bull run on the 28th, rising 17% over the previous 24-hour period. On the other hand, the S&P 500 index fell -0.25& and the Nasdaq Composite rose only marginally to +0.44%. The 60-day correlation between the S&P 500 and Bitcoin reached an all-time high in the previous week, but since the start of the war, the two assets have been trending in opposite directions.

War causes butterfly effect – crypto in spotlight as ‘store of value’

The cause of this decoupling is the turmoil the Russian war has brought to the fiat currency system. Recently, the United States, the EU, and other countries have started imposing strong economic sanctions, such as expelling belligerent Russia from the Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication (SWIFT), an international financial settlement network. These economic sanctions compound the chaos caused by the war, collapsing the fiat currency system of some countries. This has brought attention on borderless cryptocurrencies as a store of value in times of crisis.

“The Ukraine-Russia situation has caused significant financial upheaval and individuals, businesses and indeed government agencies – not just in the region but globally – are looking for alternatives to traditional systems”, said deVere Group’s Nigel Green, citing mounting financial chaos within Russia that’s affecting everyday citizens. “As banks close, ATMs run out of money, threats of personal savings being taken to pay for war, and the major international payments system SWIFT is weaponized, amongst other factors, the case for a viable, decentralized, borderless, tamper-proof, un-confiscatable monetary system has been laid bare”, Green wrote. “And as alternatives, such as crypto, prove to be credible and workable, the dollar’s reserve status could, ultimately, be in jeopardy”, he added.

According to data released by Arcane Research, cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Tether (USDT) are attracting attention as a store of value in the Ukrainian and Russian markets. On the 28th, Tether-Russian ruble (RUB) trading volume reached an all-time high of $35 million on the cryptocurrency exchange Binance, and Bitcoin trading volume also showed a significant increase. The Arcane report noted that “Russians are looking for stable assets linked to the dollar due to possible Russian sanctions” and that “Ukrainians are also seeking cryptocurrency exposure as a safe haven amid concerns that the country’s banking system could collapse”.

“When freedom and individual liberty are threatened, the price of bitcoin goes up”, said Antoni Trenchev, co-founder and managing partner of Nexo. “Alongside the overselling in the market over the previous few weeks, a rebound was inevitable”. Bloomberg Intelligence commodity strategist Mike McGlone concurred, saying: “I see bitcoin transitioning from a risk-on to risk-off asset, and so far in 2022, the crypto has made good progress on this trajectory”.

Future direction – Will Bitcoin decouple from stocks?

The S&P Index, which has been a hallmark risk asset in the past, entered a bear market during the 2008 financial crisis about 150 days after peaking in October 2007. At the time, the index took about 2,000 days to reclaim its peak in April 2013. So, how long will it take for Bitcoin to climb back to its peak after the Russian war? Will Bitcoin follow a similar trajectory as stocks going forward? As for the price outlook on cryptocurrencies, the majority opinion is to take a wait-and-see approach.

Recent Coinmetrics data shows that the 60-day correlation between the S&P 500 and Bitcoin is declining. Accordingly, experts are paying attention to the possibility that cryptocurrencies will decouple (separate) from stocks in the future and function as an independent asset class. In this event, the current way of evaluating crypto assets and predicting their price together with stocks is expected to change significantly.

Noelle Acheson, head of market insights with Genesis Trading told Yahoo Finance last Wednesday that part of Bitcoin’s performance yesterday could be attributed to a derivatives-led “short squeeze” in addition to other momentum trading factors, before adding that some indicators suggested that something deeper could be happening.

Writing to crypto media outlet Blockworks, crypto hedge fund ARK36 executive director Mikkel Mørch remarked: “The biggest crypto asset is now looking at a potential decoupling from risk assets, and it is doing so at a time of unprecedented uncertainty. […] Cash used to be king in times of crisis, but now rising inflation levels and broader macroeconomic woes make holding large amounts of cash risk in and of itself”.

Some observers predict that volatility in the cryptocurrency market will decrease in the near term as economic sanctions against Russia trigger a decoupling between Bitcoin and the stock market. Derek Lim, head of crypto insights at Bybit, noted that Bitcoin has gained some legitimacy as a hard asset, but cautioned that “whether this decoupling between BTC and the equities market is a long-term trend or merely a short-term occurrence is still pretty much up in the air. We will still need to monitor how the crypto market reacts to news in the next few days and weeks”.
